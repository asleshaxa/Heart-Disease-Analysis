About This Project

Understanding Heart Disease Through Data

Heart disease is a group of diseases related to cardiovascular diseases, manifested by a violation of the normal functioning of the heart — covering the epicardium, pericardium, myocardium, endocardium, valvular apparatus, and heart vessels.

📊 Gender vs Heart Disease

Analysis of 4,017 female and 1,846 male patients shows distinct patterns in cardiovascular risk across gender groups.

🩺 Diabetic & Stroke Correlation

Examining how diabetes and stroke status intersect with heart disease incidence across the patient population.

🌎 Race-wise Distribution

70.47% of cases are from White patients, with significant representation from Black (24.16%), Asian (2.92%), and other groups.

🚬 Smoking & Alcohol Impact

Examining behavioral risk factors — smoking and alcohol consumption — and their influence on heart disease prevalence.

Key Research Insight

According to the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute in Framingham (USA), the most important factors in the development of cardiovascular disease in humans are obesity, sedentary lifestyle, and smoking.


This dashboard was built to visualize these relationships and make them actionable for healthcare decision-making, public health communication, and individual awareness.
